Frequently Asked Questions about Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term changes in temperature, precipitation, and other atmospheric conditions on Earth. It can result from natural factors, such as volcanic eruptions and variations in solar radiation, as well as human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels and deforestation.

The primary cause of recent climate change is the increase in greenhouse gases in the atmosphere due to human activities such as burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, and natural gas), deforestation, and industrial processes. Greenhouse gases trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to global warming.

Climate change leads to a variety of environmental impacts including more frequent and severe heatwaves, storms, droughts, and flooding. It can also cause rising sea levels, loss of sea ice, and changes in ecosystems and wildlife populations. Human health, agriculture, and water resources can also be adversely affected.

Mitigating climate change involves reducing greenhouse gas emissions and enhancing carbon sinks. This can be achieved through renewable energy sources, energy efficiency, reforestation, and sustainable agriculture. Policies and international agreements, such as the Paris Agreement, also play a crucial role in addressing climate change.

Individuals can reduce their carbon footprint by using energy-efficient appliances, reducing car travel, using public transportation, recycling, and supporting sustainable practices. Additionally, advocating for climate-friendly policies and educating others about climate change are important steps.
